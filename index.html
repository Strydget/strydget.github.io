The State of Utah

Utah, known as the Beehive State, is a land of diverse landscapes, vibrant cities, and a strong economy. Here's a concise overview of key information about Utah compared to the average state in the United States:


Population:

As of the most recent data in 2022, Utah's population stands at approximately 3.4 million residents. The state's steady population growth is attributed to factors such as a high birth rate and in-migration.


Year of Incorporation:

Utah became the 45th state of the United States on January 4, 1896, marking its official statehood.


Region of the United States:

Located in the Western part of the United States, Utah is characterized by a diverse geography that includes deserts, mountains, and canyons. It shares borders with Idaho to the north, Wyoming to the northeast, Colorado to the east, Arizona to the south, and Nevada to the west.


State Economic Classification:

Utah's economic classification is notably robust, featuring a diverse economy with strengths in technology, tourism, and outdoor recreation. The state is often recognized for its business-friendly environment, low unemployment rates, and a high quality of life.


Average Income Level:

Utah boasts a median household income of approximately $74,000 per year. This figure positions the state above the national average, reflecting the economic opportunities available to residents. The state's strong economy is further highlighted by its lower-than-average unemployment rates and a booming tech sector.
