St. George

St. George, Utah, situated in the southwestern corner of the state, stands out as a city with a unique blend of desert landscapes and a thriving community. Here's a concise overview of key information about St. George:


Population:

As of the most recent data in 2022, St. George has experienced significant growth and boasts a population of approximately 92,000 residents. The city's strategic location and warm climate contribute to its appeal as a place to live, work, and explore.


Year of Incorporation:

St. George was officially incorporated on January 17, 1862, marking its establishment as a formal municipality. Over the years, it has evolved from a small pioneer town to a vibrant and growing city.


Region of the State:

Nestled in the southwestern region of Utah, St. George is the largest city in Washington County. The city is surrounded by stunning red rock formations and is known for its proximity to recreational areas such as Zion National Park and Snow Canyon State Park.


City Classification:

St. George is commonly classified as an urban center with a strong sense of community. The city offers a mix of cultural amenities, outdoor activities, and a growing economy. While it has seen substantial growth, St. George maintains a welcoming atmosphere and a commitment to preserving its natural surroundings.


Average Income Level:

St. George exhibits a solid economic foundation, with a median household income of approximately $65,000 per year. This figure positions the city favorably in comparison to the statewide average, reflecting the economic opportunities and quality of life that St. George provides for its residents.
