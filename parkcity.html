Park City

Park City, Utah, renowned for its scenic mountain vistas and world-class skiing, is a unique town nestled within the Wasatch Mountains. Here's a concise overview of key information about Park City:


Population:

As of the most recent data in 2022, Park City has a population of approximately 8,500 residents. Despite its relatively small size, the town experiences a significant influx of visitors, especially during the winter months.


Year of Incorporation:

Park City was officially incorporated on February 25, 1884, marking its establishment as a formal municipality. Its history is deeply rooted in mining, and it later evolved into a prominent hub for outdoor recreation and tourism.


Region of the State:

Situated in the northern part of Utah, Park City is located within Summit County. The town is situated approximately 32 miles east of Salt Lake City. Its mountainous surroundings and proximity to world-class ski resorts contribute to its status as a premier destination for outdoor enthusiasts.


City Classification:

Park City can be characterized as a resort suburban town with a distinctive blend of historic charm and modern amenities. While it has urbanized areas, the town retains its unique character as a resort destination, attracting visitors with its ski resorts, cultural events, and outdoor activities.


Average Income Level:

Park City is recognized for having a relatively high average income level. The median household income in Park City is approximately $105,000 per year, surpassing the state average. This is indicative of the town's appeal not only as a recreational haven but also as a residence for those seeking an elevated quality of life.
